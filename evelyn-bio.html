Evelyn Dunlap

A Life of Faith, Strength, and Unwavering Love

December 1, 1927 – January 4, 2023

Evelyn Dunlap (1927-2023)

Evelyn Dunlap was born in the quiet town of York, one of 12 children—10 daughters and two sons—blessed to Israel and Ola Dunlap. From her earliest days, Evelyn's life was rooted in family, faith, and service. She would grow to become a pillar of strength not only within her family but also in her church and wider community.

Evelyn bore and raised six children as a single mother, making countless sacrifices so that they could be nurtured with love and empowered through education. Though she left school herself to care for her children, she instilled in them the importance of learning, perseverance, and dignity. Her children's success was her joy, and their character was her true legacy.

Evelyn was a woman of profound and unwavering faith. A devout Christian, she was deeply committed to her church, serving not only as a long-standing usher but eventually as President of the Usher Board—a role she held with honor until she gracefully passed the mantle to her successor, Hunter Tate. She adored Sunday school, where her favorite teacher, Michael, inspired her spiritually. She often referred to her beloved pastor, Reverend Gregory, as her son, a testament to the deep familial bonds she formed through her faith community.

Her spiritual life was matched only by her compassion for others. Evelyn believed in serving those in need—feeding the hungry, clothing the cold, and lifting up the downtrodden. It was not uncommon to find her in the community giving away gloves, scarves, or meals with grace and humility. She treated all people with kindness and respect—never seeing race, never judging, always loving. Her faith didn't live in words alone—it lived in every action, every prayer, every warm embrace.

A gifted cook and lover of gospel music, Evelyn filled every space she entered with warmth and joy. She cherished her role as daughter, caring deeply for her parents, especially her father, Papa Israel, who lived with her until his passing. The strength she showed in caregiving was nothing short of extraordinary—a strength that was tested time and time again and yet never faltered.

Evelyn lived to be 95 years old, the longest-living of her siblings. Her life was a living testimony to endurance, dignity, and grace. Even in her final moments, she slipped away just as she lived—peacefully and gracefully.

To her youngest son, JonPierre Carlo, Evelyn entrusted the sacred task of returning home to care for her. He describes this not as a duty, but as the greatest assignment God could have ever given him. With the support of Rajeev and Brian Kelleher at NVIDIA, JonPierre was able to be at her side, where he belonged.

Son, whatever you do in life, always protect your good name. Never give anyone a reason to speak negatively of you—for your surname is all we have. Protect it.

That legacy of character and integrity lives on in him, and in the work of this foundation.

The Evelyn Dunlap Foundation, founded in her honor shortly after her passing on January 4, 2023, is a living tribute to her life's values—faith, family, service, and dignity. Every initiative, every donation, and every act of kindness done in her name continues the story of a woman who gave all she had to uplift others.

To every visitor and donor: you are now part of Evelyn's story. As you walk through the work of this foundation, may you feel the love she gave freely, the strength she carried quietly, and the faith that guided her every step.

We honor her. We remember her. We carry her light forward.

With love and gratitude,

JonPierre Carlo
